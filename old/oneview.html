OneView

2012

Role: UI/UX • IA • Visual Design • HTML/CSS

OneView is a CRM-like system that aggregates and quantifies client information.

The internal application is developed by First NZ Capital, a financial services company in Wellington, NZ.

For the first year of development, I was the only user experience and visual designer working on the project.

My job was to work with the developers to create a clean, flexible, appealing user interface that would support the company's employees with their client interactions. Responsibilities included visual design, information architecture, user interface design and testing, as well as front-end development.

Working with Agile

OneView is an Agile project. When I came on board, the team had to adjust its Agile process to integrate usability concerns.

We found the best way to do this was for me to be involved in all parts of the Agile life cycle at once. I was part of the initial requirements stage with product owners for future stories, working on wireframes and mockups for the upcoming stories, and doing front-end design in tangent with the developers for the current stories. Meanwhile, I was working toward a big picture concept I had of the product's future.

Agile methodologies were an asset to OneView's user experience.

Working on an internal app had the advantage of having our user base defined and accessible. Unfortunately, most were heavily occupied with their work and could not dedicate time to the product's development.

Agile's quick release cycle allowed us to get new features in the hands of users, so they could give us feedback based on their actual real world experiences with the product.
